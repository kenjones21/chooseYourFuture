Choose Your Future

Climate denial in the US unfortunately still has a large hold over a significant portion of the electorate. But perhaps an even bigger challenge than outright denial of the science is the soft climate denial which seems to be pervasive in our society. I believe that carbon budgets are an effective tool to combat this sort of soft denial, and they clearly indicate that we are out of time to act on climate change, if we are to avoid the very worst of its effects, and thus climate change constitutes an existential emergency we must respond to as fast as possible.

The interactive attempts to demonstrate causality between our emissions and the risks of large temperature rises and impacts. Most materials on this do not do an adequate job of demonstrating risk; a single temperature or a range of temperatures accompanies a scenario. But it is at least as important to mention the risk of catastrophe. To use an analogy, consider a particularly dangerous plane trip. Which information is more important: that on average, the plane won't crash, or that there's nonetheless a 15% chance of the plane not landing safely?

The interactive is based largely on science summarized by the IPCC, either in the AR5 Synthesis Report or the accompanying database of future scenarios. The impacts table is based on a report from the National Resource Council.
